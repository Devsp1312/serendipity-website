How it works

How Serendipity actually works.

No buzzwords. Here's the whole thing — hardware, software, privacy, and what you actually see every day.

The hardware

A small wearable that
quietly does a lot.

Built to be worn all day. The e-ink face shows a fresh QR code each morning — your bridge between the device and the app — and only costs power when it updates.

  • Raspberry Pi 5 (16GB RAM, RP3A0 compute module)
  • Microphone for ambient audio capture
  • E-ink display — fresh QR code each morning
  • LiPo battery — all-day wear
  • Translucent side window
  • Physical mute button — mutes mic and shows muted icon on e-ink
  • On-device processing — nothing sent to any cloud
E-ink only draws power when the screen updates. Your QR costs almost nothing to display all day — the battery goes where it matters.
A day with Serendipity

Daytime. Overnight. Morning.

One loop, repeated every 24 hours. No subscriptions fired in the background, no app chirping at you — just a quiet rhythm that gets smarter every day.

01

Daytime — Listen.

The wearable passively records ambient audio while you go about your day. Press mute anytime — private conversation, sensitive meeting, whatever. It only cares about topics.

02

Overnight — Think.

When plugged in, your system cleans up the audio, converts it to text, and extracts key topics. These are turned into embeddings that refine your interests and update your profile, all while keeping the data on your local hardware. Old recordings are automatically cleared out, and your profile remains consistent even if you don't sleep for a full night.

03

Morning — Nudge.

The e-ink updates with a fresh QR code. Scan it with the app to sync your profile, then get 4–5 personalized, time-aware suggestions for the day. Thumbs up, mark done, leave feedback — your suggestions get sharper over time.

The app

Your suggestions are just for you.
Your suggestions are only yours.

No profiles to scroll, no matches to swipe on, no DMs to ignore. The app shows you 4–5 practical, time-aware task suggestions a day — things that happen to put you where people who share your interests will also be.

Try rock climbing this weekend
Check out this coffee shop Friday evening
Wear red today and smile at others doing the same

Under the hood, a FastAPI backend uses cosine similarity to group people with overlapping interests, then generates a task that naturally puts them in the same place. They don't know they were matched. They just got a suggestion they liked.

Identity & privacy

Anonymous by design.

There's no "account" to create. When you first open the app it generates a random token. That's your identity — and it's the only thing the system knows about you.

  • No email, no name, no profile, no password.
  • Your device ID links to the token — never to a real identity.
  • Lose your device? Start fresh. Nothing to recover, nothing to leak.
  • The system only ever knows user1, user2, user3 — never who those people actually are.
FAQ

Questions people ask.

Does it record everything I say?

It records ambient audio during the day, but only to extract topic patterns overnight. Raw audio is auto-deleted after 2 days. You can mute the microphone at any time with a physical button — and when you do, a muted-mic icon appears on the e-ink display so it's visible to you and anyone nearby.

What if I forget to charge it?

If processing doesn't complete (short sleep, no charge), the wearable carries over your previous day's interest profile. You record the new day normally and processing catches up the next night.

Will other people see my profile?

No. The app never shows other users at all. You only see task suggestions for yourself — suggestions happen to overlap with other people's because the matching engine quietly groups similar profiles.

How are matches made without identifying me?

Your interest topics become embeddings (numeric fingerprints). The backend uses cosine similarity to find overlap between anonymous tokens — user1, user2, user3, etc. When four tokens share a strong interest, it generates a task that puts them in the same place. No names, no identities, ever.
